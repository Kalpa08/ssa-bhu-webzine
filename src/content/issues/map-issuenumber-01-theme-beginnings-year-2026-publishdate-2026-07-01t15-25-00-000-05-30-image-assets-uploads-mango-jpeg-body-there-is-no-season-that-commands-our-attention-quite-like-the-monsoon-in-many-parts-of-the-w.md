There is no season that commands our attention quite like the monsoon. In many parts of the world, and especially in the landscapes we hold close to our hearts, the monsoon is not merely a weather event; it is a profound change in the state of existence.

When the first rains strike the parched earth, a transformation occurs. The air thickens with the scent of petrichor—the smell of life reclaiming itself. The frenetic energy of the dry heat dissipates, replaced by a steady, rhythmic pulse that seems to dictate the very speed of our thoughts.

For this issue, themed "Monsoon," we wanted to explore this period of pause. The rain, in its persistent arrival, forces a certain kind of stillness upon us. It encourages us to look inward, to observe the blurring of horizons, and to find beauty in the saturation of our surroundings.

In *Interface*, we look at the chemistry of change, perhaps reflecting on how small environmental shifts cascade into global phenomena. In *Echoes* and *Canvas*, we attempt to capture the melancholic and the jubilant shades of the gray sky. And in *Journeys*, we document the movement of life that continues despite—or perhaps because of—the deluge.

The monsoon is a reminder that constant growth requires both the scorching sun and the heavy rain. We hope this collection serves as a sanctuary for you, much like a quiet porch overlooking a storm-swept field.

We hope you find clarity in the clouds.

Testing *Editor, Nahor*
