When students leave home to pursue academia, they pack their traditions into suitcases. Yet, maintaining absolute cultural authenticity in a new environment often sparks a debate between purists and pragmatists, particularly in the kitchen.

Consider the preparation of traditional dishes in university housing. The true, smoky essence of Bati Chokha relies entirely on being slowly roasted over *goitha*—dried cow dung cakes. The specific thermal properties and the aromatic smoke imparted by the goitha simply cannot be replicated by standard coal or modern gas stoves.

The debate then becomes: is a dish prepared with modern, accessible shortcuts still the same dish? Do we accept the culinary compromise of coal for the sake of convenience, or do we insist on the ancestral methods to preserve the exact memory of home?
